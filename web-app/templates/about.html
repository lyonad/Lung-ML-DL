About LungAI

"Artificial Neural Networks vs. Random Forest for Lung Cancer Risk Prediction in Pakistan: A Comparative Analysis on Small-Scale Clinical Data"

A comprehensive research platform comparing deep learning and traditional machine learning approaches for lung cancer risk assessment in resource-constrained healthcare environments.

Project Overview

Revolutionizing lung cancer screening with artificial intelligence

Mission

To answer a critical question facing healthcare in developing countries: Which AI approach is most effective when data is limited? This research compares Artificial Neural Networks (deep learning) against Random Forest (traditional machine learning) using 309 real Pakistani patient records. Our findings show that simpler models outperform complex deep learning on small datasets‚Äîachieving higher accuracy (91.94% vs 90.32%), 10x faster training, and better interpretability for clinicians.

Vision

To provide evidence-based guidance for implementing AI in resource-constrained healthcare settings. Where data is scarce, computational power is limited, and clinician trust requires interpretable models‚Äîour research demonstrates that Random Forest is the optimal choice. This enables hospitals in Pakistan and similar developing regions to deploy effective lung cancer screening without expensive infrastructure, large datasets, or complex deep learning expertise.

Research

This comparative analysis trained and evaluated 5 different architectures: Simple ANN, Deep ANN, Advanced ANN, Regularized ANN, and Random Forest‚Äîall on the same 309-patient dataset. We employed class weight balancing to handle data imbalance, ROC-based optimal threshold selection for each model, and 10-fold cross-validation for robust evaluation. Results conclusively show Random Forest's superiority on small clinical datasets.

Research & Dataset

Evidence-based approach to AI healthcare

Dataset Information & Research Context

This comparative study utilizes a lung cancer survey dataset from 309 Pakistani patients with 15 clinical and demographic features. The research specifically investigates "Artificial Neural Networks vs. Random Forest for Lung Cancer Risk Prediction in Pakistan: A Comparative Analysis on Small-Scale Clinical Data" to address a fundamental question in medical AI: Does the complexity of deep learning provide advantages when training data is limited?

Our findings reveal a critical insight for healthcare in developing countries: sophisticated deep learning architectures do not outperform traditional machine learning when datasets are small. In fact, Random Forest achieves 91.94% accuracy‚Äîsurpassing the best ANN (90.32%)‚Äîwhile requiring 10x less training time, minimal computational resources, and providing interpretable feature importance that clinicians can understand and trust.

üèÜ Key Research Finding: On small clinical datasets (n=309), Random Forest outperforms all Neural Network architectures, achieving superior accuracy (91.94% vs 85.48-90.32%), 98% precision, 92.6% sensitivity, dramatically faster training (2s vs 20-40s), lower overfitting risk, and crucial interpretability‚Äîmaking it the optimal choice for resource-constrained healthcare settings in Pakistan and similar developing regions.

Risk Factors Analyzed:

  • Demographic: Age, Gender
  • Lifestyle: Smoking, Peer Pressure
  • Physical Indicators: Yellow Fingers
  • Health Conditions: Chronic Disease, Allergies
  • Psychological: Anxiety, Fatigue
  • Respiratory Symptoms: Wheezing, Coughing, Shortness of Breath
  • Other Symptoms: Swallowing Difficulty, Chest Pain

Comparative Methodology:

To ensure a fair comparison between Artificial Neural Networks and Random Forest, we implemented a rigorous experimental design where all models were trained on identical data with identical preprocessing. This controlled approach isolates the impact of model architecture from other variables, enabling us to definitively answer which approach works best for small clinical datasets.

  • Class Weight Balancing: Critical for handling severe imbalance (87% HIGH_RISK vs 13% LOW_RISK). Without this, models would achieve high accuracy by simply predicting "cancer" for everyone‚Äîuseless clinically.
  • Optimal Threshold Selection: ROC curve analysis determined optimal decision thresholds (ANN: 0.6862, RF: 0.5467) to maximize both sensitivity (catching true cancer cases) and specificity (avoiding false alarms)‚Äîbalancing the cost of missed diagnoses vs unnecessary anxiety.
  • 5 Model Architectures Compared: We tested increasing complexity: Simple ANN (baseline) ‚Üí Deep ANN (more layers) ‚Üí Advanced ANN (wider layers) ‚Üí Regularized ANN (dropout + L2) ‚Üí Random Forest (ensemble). Result: More complexity didn't help; Random Forest won.
  • 10+ Performance Metrics: Beyond accuracy, we evaluated ROC-AUC, Matthews Correlation Coefficient, Cohen's Kappa, Balanced Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-Score, and more‚Äîensuring models perform well across multiple dimensions, not just overall accuracy.
  • Perfect LOW_RISK Detection: Random Forest achieved 100% specificity (8/8 LOW_RISK correctly identified), meaning zero false positives in the test set‚Äîcrucial for avoiding unnecessary treatment and patient anxiety in resource-limited settings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our platform

How accurate is this prediction system, and why did Random Forest outperform Neural Networks?
Our Random Forest model achieves 91.94% accuracy with 98.04% precision and 92.6% sensitivity on test data from 309 Pakistani patients. The best performing Neural Network (Regularized ANN) achieves 90.32% accuracy. This outcome challenges the common assumption that "deep learning always wins"‚Äîit doesn't when data is scarce. Why Random Forest excels: (1) It's less prone to overfitting on small datasets due to ensemble averaging, (2) It doesn't require large amounts of data to learn complex patterns, (3) It handles feature interactions naturally without needing thousands of training examples, and (4) It's robust to class imbalance with proper weighting. However, this is a screening tool, not a diagnostic replacement‚Äîalways consult qualified healthcare professionals for medical diagnosis and treatment decisions.
Is my data secure and private?
Yes. We do not store any patient data. All predictions are made in real-time and your information is not saved or shared. The data you input is only used for generating your risk assessment and is immediately discarded after results are returned.
What should I do if I receive a high-risk assessment?
A high-risk assessment means you should consult with a healthcare professional as soon as possible. They can perform proper diagnostic tests such as chest X-rays, CT scans, or biopsies. Early detection is crucial for successful treatment.
Can I use this for clinical diagnosis?
No. This tool is designed for educational and screening purposes only. It is not intended for clinical diagnosis or treatment decisions. Always consult qualified healthcare professionals for medical advice, diagnosis, and treatment.
How were the models trained and compared fairly?
To conduct a rigorous comparative analysis titled "Artificial Neural Networks vs. Random Forest for Lung Cancer Risk Prediction in Pakistan: A Comparative Analysis on Small-Scale Clinical Data", we trained 5 different architectures (Simple ANN, Deep ANN, Advanced ANN, Regularized ANN, Random Forest) on the identical dataset of 309 Pakistani patient records with 15 risk factors.

Experimental Design: (1) Class weight balancing (critical for 87% HIGH_RISK vs 13% LOW_RISK imbalance‚Äîwithout this, models would just predict "cancer" for everyone), (2) Feature scaling with StandardScaler (required for ANNs but not for RF, yet we scaled for both to maintain consistency), (3) ROC-based optimal threshold selection (individualized for each model via ROC curve analysis: ANN=0.6862, RF=0.5467), (4) 10-fold cross-validation (ensures results aren't due to lucky train/test splits), and (5) Comprehensive evaluation with 10+ metrics including ROC-AUC, Matthews Correlation Coefficient, Cohen's Kappa, and Balanced Accuracy.

Key Finding: Random Forest decisively outperformed all Neural Networks‚Äî91.94% accuracy, 10x faster training (2s vs 20-40s), superior precision (98.04%), and interpretable feature importance (clinicians can see exactly which symptoms drove each prediction). This demonstrates that for small clinical datasets (n=309), traditional machine learning beats deep learning.
Why is this research particularly important for Pakistan and similar developing countries?
This research addresses real-world constraints that healthcare systems in developing countries face: (1) Limited Data Availability‚ÄîMany hospitals don't have thousands of patient records needed for deep learning; 309 patients is often all you can collect. (2) Computational Constraints‚ÄîTraining a Random Forest takes 2 seconds on a standard laptop vs 40 seconds for Deep ANN; in facilities with limited computing power, this matters enormously. (3) Interpretability Requirements‚ÄîPakistani clinicians need to understand and trust AI recommendations; Random Forest provides clear feature importance ("age and smoking were the top factors"), while neural networks are "black boxes". (4) Cost-Effectiveness‚ÄîDeploying Random Forest requires minimal infrastructure, no GPUs, and can run on basic hardware.

Our findings prove that sophisticated AI doesn't always mean better AI‚Äîfor resource-constrained settings, simpler models are not just acceptable, they're superior. This enables Pakistani hospitals and clinics to implement effective lung cancer screening immediately, without waiting for massive datasets or expensive infrastructure.
